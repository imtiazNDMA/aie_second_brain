# Causality

The study of cause-and-effect relationships, particularly in the context of AI systems and statistical reasoning. Judea Pearl is a renowned researcher in this field.

## Definition

Causality focuses on understanding not just correlations but actual causal relationships between variables. This is critical for AI systems that need to reason about interventions, counterfactuals, and underlying mechanisms rather than just statistical patterns.

## Key Concepts

- **Causal Models**: Mathematical representations of causal relationships (e.g., Bayesian networks, structural equation models)
- **Interventions**: Actions that change the value of a variable to observe effects
- **Counterfactuals**: Reasoning about "what would have happened if..." scenarios
- **Causal Discovery**: Algorithms that learn causal structure from observational data

## Relevance to AI

- **Explainable AI**: Understanding causal mechanisms helps explain model decisions
- **Robustness**: Causal models are more robust to distribution shifts
- **Fairness**: Identifying causal vs spurious correlations helps ensure fair AI systems
- **Agentic Systems**: Agents that understand causality can reason more effectively
