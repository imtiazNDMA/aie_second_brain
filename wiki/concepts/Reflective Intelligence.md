# Reflective Intelligence

The capability of AI systems to reason about their own reasoning processes, monitor their performance, and adapt their strategies based on self-reflection.

## Definition

Reflective intelligence enables AI agents to:
- **Self-monitor**: Track their own decision-making processes
- **Self-evaluate**: Assess the quality of their outputs and reasoning
- **Self-adapt**: Modify strategies based on past performance
- **Meta-reason**: Think about thinking itself

## Applications in AI Agents

- **Feedback loops**: Agents that learn from their own mistakes
- **Confidence estimation**: Knowing when they don't know something
- **Strategy switching**: Choosing different approaches when current ones fail
- **Explanation generation**: Explaining their own reasoning to users

## Relation to Other Concepts

- **Reflection** (in agents): The capability to self-evaluate and improve
- **Reasoning Strategies**: Different approaches agents can reflect upon and choose from
- **Agentic Systems**: Systems that benefit from reflective capabilities
- **Causality**: Understanding cause-effect can enhance reflective reasoning
