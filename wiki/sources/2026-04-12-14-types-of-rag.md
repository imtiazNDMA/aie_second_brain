# 14 Types of RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

**Author:** Meilisearch content team  
**Source:** https://www.meilisearch.com/blog/rag-types  
**Date ingested:** 2026-04-12  
**Type:** article

## Summary

Survey of fourteen retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) architectures, ranging from naïve one-shot retrievers to agentic, graph-based, corrective, and speculative pipelines. For each pattern the article outlines mechanics, typical use cases, and pros/cons, then closes with tooling recommendations (Meilisearch, LangChain, Weaviate, Faiss, Haystack). The taxonomy underscores that no single RAG stack fits every workload—teams must balance speed, reasoning depth, memory, and governance requirements.

## Key Claims

- The “simple”/“naïve” RAG baseline—single retrieval pass + generation—is easy to deploy but fails on multi-step reasoning, motivating richer variants (memory, branched, corrective, adaptive, speculative, modular).
- Agentic and graph-driven RAGs behave like researchers: they plan, explore relationships, and iterate until an answer satisfies the intent, trading latency/cost for completeness.
- Self-reflective patterns ([[Self-RAG]], Corrective RAG, HyDE) rewrite queries, critique answers, or generate hypothetical documents to boost precision on weak/ambiguous questions.
- Tooling choices (vector stores, orchestration frameworks) must align with the architecture; modular RAG benefits from swappable retrievers, rerankers, and generators so teams can evolve components independently.

## Structure

- **Simple & Naïve RAG** – Basic retrieve-once designs; pros/cons of speed vs accuracy.
- **Memory-Augmented & Branched** – Context persistence plus multiple interpretation branches for conversational agents.
- **Agentic & Adaptive** – Planner-style systems that decompose problems, reason iteratively, and adjust retrieval depth.
- **Graph RAG** – Knowledge graphs to model relationships across entities/topics.
- **[[Self-RAG]] / Corrective / HyDE** – Self-reflective pipelines that rewrite queries, double-check answers, or generate hypotheticals.
- **Speculative & Multimodal** – Prefetching likely follow-ups and ingesting mixed media (text, images, video).
- **Modular & Advanced** – Toolkit-style architectures layering rerankers, feedback loops, and memory; includes summary of RAG tooling landscape.
